## KEY PATTERNS FOR F-PRIME INTERCEPTION STRATEGY

### 1. MIT EECS Pipeline is the #1 Source
- 6 out of 8 companies have founders with MIT EECS connections
- The Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT is the single most productive pipeline

### 2. Class of 2018-2022 Was Extraordinarily Productive
- Anysphere: Class of 2022
- Kalshi: Class of 2018/2019
- Codeium: Class of ~2019 (estimated)
- Monitoring MIT EECS graduating classes is high-value

### 3. "Founded While at MIT" Pattern
- Anysphere (Cursor) and Kalshi were both founded while founders were still MIT students
- This is the strongest interception signal — identify students who are building companies during their MIT years

### 4. SuperUROP and Research Programs are Signals
- Tarek Mansour (Kalshi) participated in MIT SuperUROP (deep learning for breast cancer)
- Students in advanced research programs who are thinking about startups are high-priority targets

### 5. Neo Scholars as a Feeder Program
- Anysphere founders were Neo Scholars
- Ali Partovi's Neo program serves as a pre-filter for technical founders

### 6. International Math/CS Olympiad Winners at MIT
- Multiple Cursor founders had International Olympiad backgrounds (Informatics, Math)
- These competition winners who attend MIT are high-signal for future technical founders

### 7. MIT→Stanford PhD Pipeline
- Cody Coleman: MIT BS → Stanford PhD (with Matei Zaharia) → Coactive AI
- Tracking MIT undergrads who go to top PhD programs is a long-term interception strategy

### 8. Y Combinator Connection
- Scale AI (YC S16) and Kalshi (YC W19) both went through Y Combinator
- MIT students applying to YC is a compounding signal
