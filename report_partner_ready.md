Brian Halligan posted that 21 of the Forbes AI 50 companies had founders who went to school in Boston/Cambridge. We traced every founder back to their campus, lab, and career waypoint. The conclusion is uncomfortable: **F-Prime is headquartered in the most productive AI founder ecosystem on the planet, and most of these founders were within a 15-minute walk of our office.**

Here's what we would have gotten if we'd been positioned correctly:

These aren't hypotheticals. These founders were physically in Cambridge, taking classes, presenting research, and building prototypes. The window was open.

## The 21 Companies: Deal-Level Analysis

### Tier 1: "Should Have Been Our Deal" — Founded in Cambridge

These companies were literally started in our backyard. No flight required.

**1. Anysphere (Cursor)* — $29.3B**
- 4 MIT CS undergrads, Class of '22. Neo Scholars. Incorporated at MIT in 2022.
- **The miss**: $400K pre-seed in early 2022 while still on campus. OpenAI's accelerator got them in 2023.
- **Signal we should have caught**: Neo Scholars cohort + all four had Olympiad medals (IOI, IMO, USACO). Our portfolio company Benchling has MIT founders — a warm intro path existed.

**2. Etched* — $3.3B**
- 3 Harvard undergrads (Uberti '25, Zhu '24, Wachen '24). Dorm room project Oct 2022. Seed closed Apr 2023. All became Thiel Fellows.
- **The miss**: 6-month window (Oct 2022 - Apr 2023) while taking Harvard math/CS classes. Primary Venture Partners got the seed.
- **Signal we should have caught**: They wrote a paper showing 140x improvement over GPUs. Harvard CS students publishing technical papers outside of coursework is a screaming signal.

**3. Suno — Cambridge HQ, $125M Series B**
- 3 of 4 founders are Harvard (Shulman PhD Physics, Kucsko PhD Physics, Camacho BS CS graduated at 18). All 4 worked at Kensho in Cambridge. **Suno is still headquartered in Cambridge, MA.**
- **The miss**: The entire founding team worked at Kensho — 2 miles from our office — from 2015-2022. Mikey Shulman has been an MIT Sloan lecturer since 2018. Lightspeed got the Series B.
- **Signal we should have caught**: When Kensho was acquired by S&P Global for $550M in 2018, the top ML talent stayed temporarily. Tracking their departures should have been automatic.

**4. Scale AI* — $14.3B**
- Alexandr Wang, MIT dropout (~2016). Took 5 grad courses in ~1 year. Parents were Los Alamos nuclear physicists.
- **The miss**: Very narrow window — he was at MIT for only ~1 year before entering YC S16. But he was already working at Quora as a teenager pre-MIT — the signal was there.
- **Signal we should have caught**: Teenager working at Quora who enrolls at MIT and immediately starts taking grad-level courses. Extreme outlier.

**5. Kalshi — $3B+**
- Tarek Mansour (MIT BS '18, MNG '19, SuperUROP in deep learning) and Luana Lopes Lara (MIT BS CS '18, former Bolshoi ballerina turned CS). Founded at MIT 2018, entered YC W19.
- **The miss**: Both were MIT EECS, founding the company in their final year. They left for YC shortly after.
- **Signal we should have caught**: Tarek did SuperUROP on deep learning. Students in advanced research programs who are also building companies are the highest-signal group.

## The Kensho Effect: F-Prime's Biggest Missed Pipeline

This deserves its own section because it's the most actionable finding.

**Daniel Nadler** founded Kensho Technologies in Cambridge in 2013 while a Harvard PhD student. It was an AI company for financial analytics. S&P Global acquired it for **$550 million** in 2018.

**What happened next**: The top talent at Kensho — all based in Cambridge — eventually left to start new companies:
- **Mikey Shulman** (Head of ML at Kensho) → co-founded **Suno** (Cambridge)
- **Georg Kucsko** (Kensho) → co-founded **Suno**
- **Martin Camacho** (Chief Architect at Kensho) → co-founded **Suno**
- **Keenan Freyberg** (Head of Strategy at Kensho) → co-founded **Suno**
- **Harrison Chase** (possibly Kensho) → co-founded **LangChain**
- **Daniel Nadler** himself → founded **OpenEvidence**

**This is 3 Forbes AI 50 companies from one Cambridge office.**

**The playbook F-Prime should run**: Identify the current Cambridge AI companies that are concentrating elite technical talent (the "next Kensho"). When those companies get acquired or talent starts departing, the founding teams of the next wave are forming. We should be mapping these companies and maintaining relationships with the key technical leaders inside them.

**Candidates for "next Kensho" monitoring**: Any Cambridge/Boston AI company with 50-200 employees, strong Harvard/MIT hiring, and recent acquisition or late-stage funding.

## What F-Prime Should Do Now

### Immediate (Next 30 Days)

1. **Assign an MIT EECS scout** — Someone (associate or intern) who attends EECS seminars, SuperUROP presentations, and MIT delta v Demo Day. Not once — every semester.

2. **Assign a Harvard CS scout** — Same for Harvard SEAS. Attend research showcases, i-lab events, and track the NLP/AI lab publications.

3. **Map the Kensho alumni network** — Pull everyone who worked at Kensho/S&P Global's AI division in Cambridge from 2013-2022. Where are they now? Who's restless? Who just left?

4. **Pull the current Neo Scholars list** — Cursor's founders were Neo Scholars. This is a curated list of the best technical undergrads at MIT. Get it.

### Ongoing (Quarterly Cadence)

5. **Track IOI/IMO/USACO medalists at MIT and Harvard** — Multiple founders in this analysis (Cursor, Perplexity, Together AI) were international competition medalists. Cross-reference competition results with MIT/Harvard enrollment.

6. **Monitor Harvard Physics → ML career pivots** — Suno's founders came from the Yacoby Lab and Lukin Group. The physics-to-ML pipeline is real and underappreciated.

7. **Attend MIT Sloan entrepreneurship events** — Suno's Mikey Shulman is an MIT Sloan lecturer. Databricks' Matei Zaharia was MIT CSAIL faculty. The faculty-entrepreneur overlap is a high-signal venue.

8. **Build a Thiel Fellowship watchlist for Harvard** — Etched (2024 class) and Speak (2012 class) founders were Harvard → Thiel Fellows. When a Harvard student gets a Thiel Fellowship, we should be calling within 48 hours.

### Structural (Next 6 Months)

9. **Create an "Intercepting Founders" CRM pipeline** — A dedicated Affinity list for pre-company technical talent at MIT and Harvard. Not companies — people. Students, PhD candidates, and researchers who show founder signals.

10. **Leverage our existing portfolio for warm intros** — Toast (MIT founders, Boston HQ), Benchling (MIT founder), and Optimus Ride (MIT professor) give us warm paths into MIT. Nebula Genomics (George Church, Harvard) gives us a path into Harvard. We should be using these relationships systematically.

11. **Host a "Cambridge AI Founders" dinner** — Quarterly. Invite technical leaders from Cambridge AI companies, MIT/Harvard faculty with startup involvement, and our portfolio founders. Create the network before people start companies, not after.
