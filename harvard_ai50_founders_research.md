### KEY PATTERNS FOR F-PRIME:

1. **Kensho as a talent nexus**: Daniel Nadler's Kensho (founded at Harvard, based in Cambridge) was a breeding ground — 4 Suno founders + possibly Harrison Chase all passed through Kensho. This is the single most important pipeline.

2. **Harvard Physics → AI founders**: Both Suno founders (Shulman from Yacoby Lab, Kucsko from Lukin Group) came from Harvard's elite quantum physics labs. The physics-to-ML pipeline is real.

3. **Harvard NLP Group (Alexander Rush)**: Demi Guo (Pika) was a star student in Rush's NLP lab. Monitoring students in this group is high-signal.

4. **Thiel Fellowship as signal**: Etched founders (2024 class) and Connor Zwick (2012 class) were Thiel Fellows. Harvard students who receive Thiel Fellowships are extreme outlier founders.

5. **Cambridge, MA as the base**: Suno remains headquartered in Cambridge. All other companies had their founding moments while founders were physically in Cambridge. F-Prime's Boston location is a structural advantage.

6. **The "graduated at 18" signal**: Martin Camacho (Suno) graduated from Harvard at age 18. Prodigious early achievements are a pattern.

7. **Accelerators**: Jesse Zhang (Decagon) went through **Y Combinator** with Lowkey. Connor Zwick went through the **Thiel Fellowship**. Etched founders went through **Thiel Fellowship**. Harvard i-lab was mentioned by Zwick as helpful.
